About

Welcome to the Physics Simulator! There are many simulations to help you on your physics journey,
try as many as you want. This website is also inspired by the google pixel phone/material ui so
thanks to google for the inspiration. Thanks to PHET for some of the ideas, and thanks to gemini
for like 15% of the math required. And lastly, thanks to you for using this simulator! Enjoy!

Projectile Motion

Simulate the path of a projectile under gravity, including launch angle and velocity.

Pendulum

Investigate the motion of a simple pendulum and how length and gravity affect its period.

Atwood Machine

Visualize two masses connected by a string over a pulley and analyze their acceleration.

Circular Motion

Explore uniform circular motion and centripetal force with adjustable parameters.

Newton's Second Law

Experiment with mass, force, and acceleration to see Newton's Second Law in action.

Friction

Explore static and kinetic friction and how they affect motion on different surfaces.

Free Fall

Drop objects from different heights and compare their motion with and without air resistance.

Simple Harmonic Motion

Visualize spring-mass oscillations and explore how mass, spring constant, and damping affect motion.

Conservation of Momentum

Simulate elastic and inelastic collisions on a track and observe momentum conservation.

Collisions

Watch elastic collisions between balls with different masses and observe momentum conservation.

Work and Energy

Explore the relationship between work, kinetic energy, and potential energy through friction.

Conservation of Energy

Track energy transformations in systems with springs, ramps, and pendulums.

Power

Calculate the rate of doing work in different physical scenarios with forces and motion.

Electric Circuits

Build and analyze basic series and parallel circuits with bulbs and batteries.

Wave Motion

Explore sinusoidal wave propagation with adjustable amplitude, frequency, and wavelength.

Sound Waves

Visualize longitudinal sound waves and how frequency and amplitude affect pitch and loudness.

Center of Mass

Visualize how center of mass is calculated for multiple objects and how it moves.

Rotational Kinematics

Explore angular displacement, velocity, and acceleration for rotating objects in circular motion.
